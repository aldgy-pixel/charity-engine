# Council: Parallel probes or serialize Probe B first — 4-action ceiling forces a choice

## The decision
Both Probe A (wellness studio outreach, 50 contacts) and Probe B (Upwork application sprint, 15 apps) launched 2026-05-28 with $0 revenue and 0 hours logged. The kill date is 2026-06-11 — 13 days away. Real capacity is ~1 hr/day with a hard cap of 2 Charity Engine actions/week through June 30, yielding at most 4 CE actions before expiry. Each probe needs 3–4 focused hours to produce interpretable signal; running both in parallel consumes all 4 actions and leaves each probe half-baked. The decision: hold the parallel-probe design and split actions 2+2, or serialize with Probe B first (2+0 then 0+2) and let Upwork signal land before spending hours on studio research.

## Five lenses

### Contrarian — what's wrong / will fail
Serializing Probe B is just rationalizing the path of least resistance — Upwork is the obvious safe choice and will seduce the engine into a $50/hr ceiling that never compounds toward $10K. The parallel design exists precisely to force a fair comparison rather than defaulting to the lowest-friction option. Letting Upwork win by default isn't a test, it's a surrender.

### First Principles — is this even the right question?
The real question is: what minimum signal does each probe need to produce a confident decision by June 11? Probe B needs 1 contract offer or 3 active interviews — achievable from a single 2-hour application sprint. Probe A needs 1 closed call or 3 booked discoveries — but the wellness sales cycle is 3–6 weeks, meaning even a "win" signal by June 11 doesn't produce charity dollars until July at earliest. The probes aren't symmetric in time-to-dollar, which makes the parallel design less meaningful than it looks.

### Expansionist — what upside is being undervalued?
Probe B at $50–60/hr with a contract in hand by mid-June means real charity dollars in Q3, not Q4. That's 3–4 months of compounding lead time. Every week of dithering between probe designs is a week of lost Upwork earnings. Probe A has a longer sales cycle even in the best case — a wellness studio discovery call booked in week 2 still closes in week 4 at the earliest, and charity dollars don't land until after an invoice clears.

### Outsider — what would a fresh reader find confusing or missing?
A fresh reader would note that the ledger shows 0 hours on both probes the day after launch, with a 13-day clock running — no start action has been taken yet. The "2 CE actions/week" cap is stated in the project doc but there's no committed time slot for either probe, which means client deadlines will fill every available hour by default. The parallel probe design reads as a plan, not a commitment.

### Executor — what breaks Monday morning?
Without a specific calendar block before June 5, both probes stay at 0 hours. Probe B has a clear atomic unit of work — 15 Upwork applications, ~2 hours, done. Probe A's first action ("identify 50 warm-adjacent studios") is open-ended research with no obvious endpoint, making it easy to defer. If both compete for the same 2-action budget this week, the vaguer task loses — meaning Probe A gets 0 actions and the parallel design is fiction anyway.

## Verdict

- **Where lenses agree:** The parallel design is practically unworkable at 2 CE actions/week; Probe B has a faster time-to-dollar and a lower-activation-energy first action; something needs to start before June 5 or the test expires with zero data.
- **Where lenses clash:** The Contrarian correctly warns that "Probe B first" risks becoming "Probe B only" — the safer path crowds out the higher-ceiling bet without a firm commitment to run Probe A after.
- **Recommendation:** Serialize — run Probe B's application sprint (2-hour block, 15 apps) by June 5, then immediately use any remaining capacity before June 11 for Probe A's studio outreach — but treat Probe B as the tiebreaker: if it hits its win condition by June 8, kill Probe A and commit.
- **One thing to do first:** Block a 2-hour slot on the calendar before June 5 and submit 15 Upwork applications — no studio research until that sprint is done.
