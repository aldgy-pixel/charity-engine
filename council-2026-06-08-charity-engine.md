# Council: Fork test closes in 72 hrs — call the verdict and set the post-June-11 plan

## The decision

The 14-day fork test (started 2026-05-28, kills 2026-06-11) is 3 days from its deadline. The ledger shows 0 hours and $0 revenue on both probes. The June 1 council recommended killing Probe A and committing all remaining hours to Probe B (Upwork), with a specific action item: "Monday June 2, write 3 applications." It is now June 8. Either that work was done and never logged, or it wasn't done. Either way, the fork test will close on June 11 with no logged signal, Karavay deadline lands June 13, and FreePrints runs to June 30. The decision: call the fork test verdict now rather than waiting for June 11 — is this a market failure or a capacity failure — and set the concrete post-June-11 plan before the date arrives.

## Five lenses

### Contrarian — what's wrong / will fail

The pattern is already the answer: two councils in a row made the same diagnosis (not enough hours, need to focus), both produced a "one thing to do Monday morning," and neither got logged in the ledger. A third council is not going to change the execution pattern. The fork test didn't fail because the probes were poorly designed — it failed because client deadlines consumed every available hour. A post-June-11 plan that assumes July brings more capacity will hit the same wall if FreePrints runs to June 30 and no concrete calendar block is reserved.

### First Principles — is this even the right question?

The fork test was designed to pick the faster path to a charity dollar, and the June 1 council already answered it: Probe B (Upwork) is the path, Probe A was killed. The "14-day test" framing is now a formality — there's nothing to compare. The real question isn't "which probe won" but "when does Probe B actually run, and under what conditions?" The 14-day test was a decision-forcing mechanism, not a market experiment. Extending or re-running the test changes nothing unless the capacity constraint changes first.

### Expansionist — what upside is being undervalued?

The client deadline season that killed the fork test is itself proof of concept: Alex is billable. Karavay and FreePrints are paying clients. The hour rate on those engagements, compared against Upwork's $45–60/hr target, is worth surfacing — if existing clients pay more per hour than Upwork targets, the Charity Engine play might be to raise rates on client work and route the delta to charity, not to find new Upwork clients. July 1 also unlocks 15–30 hrs/week, which is enough to run a real Probe B sprint: 15 applications in one week, interviews within two weeks, first contract potentially by July 15. The test didn't fail; it got deferred to better conditions.

### Outsider — what would a fresh reader find confusing or missing?

A cold reader looking at this ledger — 11 days in, two councils, 0 hours logged, kill deadline in 72 hours — would ask: has any work at all happened? The ledger is Alex's source of truth, and it says zero. If Upwork applications were written on June 2 and not logged, the council system is blind to real progress. The outsider confusion is: it's impossible to distinguish "nothing happened" from "something happened and wasn't recorded." Either the ledger discipline needs to be enforced as a hard rule, or the council's information is structurally unreliable.

### Executor — what breaks Monday morning (June 9)?

Monday June 9: 3 days until kill deadline, 4 days until Karavay due. If this council's output is "run more Probe B before June 11," there's realistically 1–2 hours across the next 3 days — enough for 1–2 Upwork applications, not enough to hit 3 active interviews. If the output is "declare the test inconclusive and replan for July," Monday's action is to calendar-block the first Probe B sprint week in July (July 7–11) with specific daily targets. The second option takes 10 minutes and creates a concrete commitment; the first risks burning the last 3 hours on applications that won't produce signal before the deadline anyway.

## Verdict

- **Where lenses agree:** The fork test will close on June 11 without hitting either probe's win conditions. This is a capacity failure, not a market failure — client deadlines crowded out Charity Engine hours. The path forward is Probe B (Upwork), not a re-examination of the premise.
- **Where lenses clash:** Contrarian says a third council won't fix the execution pattern and calls for a structural change (calendar blocks, ledger discipline); Expansionist says July unlocks real capacity and the test outcome is fine to defer. Both are right — the deferred test only matters if July is actually protected.
- **Recommendation:** Declare the 14-day test inconclusive on June 11, confirm Probe B as the surviving path, and treat July 7 as the real start date for a clean Probe B sprint with actual capacity.
- **One thing to do first:** Before June 11, calendar-block July 7–18 as "Probe B sprint — 2 hrs/day, 15 Upwork applications, check for 1 contract or 3 interviews by July 18." Write it down somewhere Alex will actually see it, not just in the ledger.
