# Council: Drop one probe now — 10 hrs left, 2 probes need 10 hrs each

## The decision

Both probes (Probe A: wellness offer, Probe B: Upwork benchmark) sit at 0 hours, 0 revenue, 4 days into a 14-day test. Remaining capacity before the June 11 kill date is approximately 10 hours (1 hr/day × 10 days). Each probe realistically requires 5–10 hours to produce a meaningful signal: Probe A needs studio identification, mockup creation, and outreach; Probe B needs 15 high-quality applications. Running both in parallel at this capacity means neither gets enough fuel to hit win conditions. The decision: drop one probe now and give the remaining 10 hours to one clear path, or accept that both will likely fail the June 11 test by underinvestment.

## Five lenses

### Contrarian — what's wrong / will fail

Running both probes on 1 hr/day will produce two half-hearted attempts, neither of which generates signal — you'll hit June 11 with 5 hours each, zero wins on both, and conclude "neither path works" when the real lesson is "neither path got tested." Parallel probes require parallel capacity. You don't have it. Splitting 10 hours is not a 14-day test; it's a 7-day non-test run twice.

### First Principles — is this even the right question?

The fork test was designed to pick the faster path to a charity dollar. That selection criterion points directly at Probe B (Upwork): cash-in-hand by week 2–3, near-zero sales risk, no mockup creation overhead. If the actual goal is fastest path to revenue — which is what $10K by 2027-10-04 requires — the fork test framing already answered itself. The question isn't "which probe gets the hours" but "why is Probe A still in the running against a path that is objectively faster to first dollar?"

### Expansionist — what upside is being undervalued?

Probe B's upside is routinely underpriced: Upwork at $45–60/hr with 15 applications in a focused sprint is a high-probability outcome, not a bet. A single contract closes the first month's charity-earmark quota immediately. If one contract at 10 hrs/week generates $600/week, the $10K goal is achievable in ~9 months of part-time work — well inside the 16-month window — without touching Probe A at all. The wellness productized service is a higher-ceiling but lower-probability play that Alex may never have time to build during client-deadline season.

### Outsider — what would a fresh reader find confusing or missing?

A cold reader would ask: if the ledger shows 0 hours after 4 days and the kill date is June 11, has the test actually started? The "14-day test" framing implies structured daily execution, but the ledger and next-actions ("identify 50 studios," "sprint 15 applications") are still pre-execution. There's no hourly or daily log, no intermediate checkpoint, no signal that either probe has generated a single data point. From the outside, this looks like planning a test, not running one.

### Executor — what breaks Monday morning?

Monday morning: 1 hour available. If both probes are still "active," the first decision of the day is which probe to work on — and that meta-decision consumes the hour. If Probe B is the surviving probe, Monday's action is specific and executable: open Upwork, write 2–3 high-quality applications, close laptop. Specificity beats strategy when capacity is 1 hr/day. Dual-probe status creates decision fatigue that has already cost 4 days.

## Verdict

- **Where lenses agree:** 10 hours cannot properly fund two 10-hour probes. Every lens points toward concentration, not parallelism, at this capacity level.
- **Where lenses clash:** Contrarian says both will fail; Expansionist says Probe B alone is sufficient for the $10K goal. There's no real tension — they agree Probe B is the surviving bet; they differ only on whether Probe A was ever feasible at 1 hr/day.
- **Recommendation:** Kill Probe A now. Commit all remaining June capacity (≈10 hrs before June 11, then ≈1 hr/day through June 30) to Probe B — Upwork applications only.
- **One thing to do first:** Monday, write and submit 3 Upwork applications for React/frontend work at $45–60/hr. Log hours in the ledger. That is the only Charity Engine action this week.
