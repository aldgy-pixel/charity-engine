# Council: July 7 plan cancelled — what does the structural reset actually look like?

## The decision

The June 15 verdict set a bright-line condition: if the ledger shows zero hours at the June 22 council, the July 7 Upwork sprint plan is cancelled and a full strategic reset is required. Today is June 22. The ledger shows 0 hours logged, $0 revenue, $0 to charity — identical to every prior week since launch on May 28. That condition is met. The decision is not whether to reset (it is mandatory per June 15), but what a structural reset looks like when five consecutive councils have each produced a good plan and zero logged execution.

## Five lenses

### Contrarian — what's wrong / will fail

Five councils, five zero-weeks, five plans that failed to produce a single logged hour. This is not bad luck — it's structural. Every plan required Alex to choose Charity Engine work over paid client work during a constrained window, and paid work won every time. That is rational, not a character flaw. A reset that still requires opening Upwork cold, writing applications on spec, and waiting weeks for a contract is the same plan with a new date. The July 7 plan was already failing before it started — Karavay freed up June 13 and produced zero Upwork applications in 9 days. Changing the start date has been tried four times.

### First Principles — is this even the right question?

The terminal goal is $10,000 deposited to charity by 2027-10-04. The mechanism chosen was "find new freelance clients via Upwork." But Alex already has a paying client — FreePrints, active through June 30 at an hourly rate presumably comparable to the $45–60/hr Upwork target. First principles: the engine is already running; it just hasn't been attached to the charity formula. The question isn't "how do we start the engine?" — it's "why haven't we applied the charity meter to income that is already billing?" That is a five-minute ledger entry, not a new strategy session.

### Expansionist — what upside is being undervalued?

FreePrints closes June 30. If Alex has billed even 20 hours at $50/hr, that's $1,000 gross. At 50% charity routing post-tax (30% tax assumption): $1,000 × 0.70 × 0.50 = $350 earmarked — already earned and not yet counted. Log it in the ledger and the zero streak breaks today, the formula is proven on real revenue, and the remaining ~470 days have a working reference point. Five weeks of existing paid work could already count toward the $10K goal but remain invisible because nobody attached the charity formula to it. The June 12 council explicitly said: "if FreePrints rate exceeds $50/hr, the Charity Engine math applies to new client work immediately." That directive was never executed either.

### Outsider — what would a fresh reader find confusing or missing?

A cold reader opens the ledger and sees both Probe A and Probe B still listed as `status: active` despite being killed on June 12. The ledger is frozen at the May 28 launch state — five councils of decisions and reversals are invisible in the only tracking document that is supposed to show what's happening. That means there is no proof-of-life signal possible, because the instrument is broken. The cold reader also asks: FreePrints is a paying engagement — why isn't it in the ledger as revenue and hours? To a fresh reader, it looks like the engine has never run; to Alex's bank account, it has been running the whole time.

### Executor — what breaks Monday morning (June 23)?

FreePrints still has first call on Alex's time through June 30 — that is not changing. What is actionable Monday morning is not new prospecting; it is bookkeeping: close Probe A and Probe B with kill-criteria postmortems, open a FreePrints bet entry with hours billed to date, apply the charity formula, and update the running totals. That takes 20 minutes and produces a non-zero ledger. Monday does not require a new strategy — it requires five weeks of accounting debt closed in one sitting.

## Verdict

- **Where lenses agree:** The reset must eliminate the "new work before first dollar" phase. Every failed plan required generating new income from scratch before the engine started. The fix is attaching the charity meter to income that is already flowing — FreePrints — rather than waiting for a new Upwork contract.
- **Where lenses clash:** Contrarian says the execution pattern is structurally irreparable and another plan will fail the same way; First Principles says the engine has been running (FreePrints) and just needs to be labeled. Both are right — but labeling FreePrints as Bet #3 requires only bookkeeping, not willpower or a new client, which makes it a different class of action than every prior plan.
- **Recommendation:** Declare FreePrints the active Charity Engine bet immediately. Close Probe A and Probe B in the ledger with postmortems, apply the 50% post-tax charity formula to all FreePrints hours billed to date, and update the running totals. The engine has been running for weeks — recognize it.
- **One thing to do first:** Open `charity_engine_ledger.md` tonight, close Probe A and Probe B (status: killed), add a FreePrints bet entry with hours billed and charity earmark calculated, and update the running totals. Twenty minutes of bookkeeping breaks a five-week zero streak and proves the formula on real revenue before any new client is needed.
